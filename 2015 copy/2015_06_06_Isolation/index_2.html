Isolation, Davis and Stairs Mountain

6/6/15 - 6/7/15

I've always loved Mt Isolation, and I've always been intrigued with how the Davis path seemed to stretch on forever into the wilderness past it when I've hiked it from the north. The northern Davis path seemed remote for a hike in the whites, the kind of place where you feel like you are just as likely to run into a moose as a person and I imagined it ran on like that for miles and miles. I always wanted to see that trail.

Finally this weekend I saw that trail. We started on the Rocky Branch trail, up to the first shelter (the path between first and second was closed or we would have done the loop), up Stairs Col Trail to the Davis path. It was just what I hoped it would be... although perhaps a little muddier and with more blowdowns. It was Jaclyn's first time to Isolation, my first backpack there (although 20 miles round trip is doable, it's a lot more fun as an overnight) and my first hike with my new camera! We ran into one couple on the way there and one couple on the way back, there were no moose, but we clearly saw the fresh prints of a mother and calf (that weren't there the prior day) and lots of droppings. It was just what I hoped it would be, a wonderful stretch of seldom seen wilderness.
